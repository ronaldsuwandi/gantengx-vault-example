### Bold-unbold technique
Steps for the bold-unbold technique
1. Write down in a document, detailed notes on things learned. **Bold** them
2. Review the list before playing
3. After playing, come back to the notes and **unbold** things that I remembered and used during playing and leave ones that I forgot bolded. Usually 30% getts unbolded
4. Before playing, review **only the ones that were bolded**
5. Repeat, after session, come back and unbold the ones that remembered

This can be applied to chess
1. Open a document, write down the concepts learned. **Bold them**
2. Review the list before playing
3. After session, review the list. Unbold the concept used and remembered, leave the bold ones there
4. Before starting next session, review the list **only the bold ones** and pay attention to those
5. Rinse and repeat

The bigger the list the better, because that means learning more new things

**Opening**
* Play aggressively
* **h4,h5**
* Castle long if "d" file is half open
* Knights first
* Don't play twice with the same piece
* **Can attack without finishing the whole development**
* TV concept
* Room concept
* Accumulate small advantages
* **Don't play useless a3,h3,a6,h6 moves**
* **Ask key questions**
* Sacrifice easily, if you win 2+ tempos
* **Exploit weak d5 square with Bb5-Bc4**
* What's my next move technique
* Am I ahead or behind

**Middlegame**
- Don't attack without reason
- Open the game with bishop pair
- Use half-open files for rooks
- **Exploit weak squares with 3 vs 2 concept**
- Thnk about counterattack
- Exchange the defenders when attacking
- **The question before resigning**
- **Avoid exchanges when lost**
- With op. castle sac "h" pawn > sac "g" pawn
- **Talk with pieces**
- **Get rid of weaknesses**
- Rook vs minor piece: exchange pieces
- Minor pieces vs rook: find outposts
- Team play of Q+N
- **R+B > R+N**

**Endgame**
- Get a queen
- Create a passed pawn
- Win pawns
- **Philidor position - short defense**
- **Don't castle quickly**
- Exchange a few pawns with B vs N
- **Avoid unnecessary weaknesses**
- Put your pawns on the opposite color of your bishop
- Bishop pair is stronger in the end game
- **Rook endgame - keep connected passed pawn**
- Rook endgame - 2 vs 1 is a draw
- Pawn endgame - square rule
- **Can attack!**
- **Don't forget about tactics**
- **Play with purpose**
