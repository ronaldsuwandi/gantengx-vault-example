## Performance
Focus on the input and not rating/outcome. Keep track of the indicators (KPI)
- Concentration
- Energy
- Sharp mind

Pay attention to those when playing chess. Don't play when under heavy emotion

At the end of each session, grade the KPI, from 1-10 in each of them. This will help me focus on the input and not worry about the outcome

All we can control was input. Output wasn’t in my hands.  But by maximizing my input, I was actually controlling the output as well.

## Joy
While playing chess, if there are more negative emotions than positive ones, eventually, no matter how much you love chess, your supply of passion for chess will be over.

Regardless of the quantity of this supply, you’ll go bankrupt sooner or later, it will definitely happen.

Positive emotions should always be bigger than negative ones.

**When you win, you should always enjoy it more than when you lose.**

After winning a game, don’t bother your mind with different tasks. Don’t check who your next opponent is, don’t prepare for the next game. Take time to enjoy your victory. Let your brain have fun. Replenishing your positive emotional supply is absolutely necessary.

Be like a bear! As a bear collects fat to survive the winter, we should collect all positive emotions and save them in our consciousness. That’s the key to having a happy journey in chess, in life, and everywhere.

Train yourself to make this a habit. At least smile and acknowledge each time something nice happens to you. You win a game, save a lost position, or win a tournament, take time to enjoy these moments.

## Stamina
For online sessions, don’t play when you feel tired. If you want to be close to chess: solve chess puzzles, read a book, watch a course. But please don’t play.

For over-the-board tournaments, ideally, you should avoid this problem before it arises by working on your physical stamina.

## Mood
Whenever during the game you find yourself in a bad mood, or you aren’t enjoying the game or your position, you straighten your back and smile

This actually helps the neuron on the brain and can help you to think clearer

## Quotes
Important quotes related with chess psychology and avoiding blunder
- No matter how winning the position is… Never ever laugh, relax, smile, or do anything that can harm focus. The game is over only when it’s really over, not when you think it’s over
- Your chess rating is the **shadow** of your level in chess. Become better and your rating will follow you.
- There’s no such thing as pressure. **It's only your ego**.
- You can control your input, but not your output. But by controlling your input you influence the output.
- If you notice a move that potentially finishes the game, don’t do it immediately. It’s a critical moment. Double or triple-check it. Take your time.
